Learn Sign Language

Discover how to communicate using American Sign Language (ASL) alphabet signs. These hand gestures represent individual letters and form the foundation of sign language communication.

Getting Started with QuickSign

QuickSign is designed to make learning and using sign language easier for everyone. Our AI-powered system recognizes hand gestures in real-time and translates them into text.

How to Use QuickSign:

  1. Position your hand: Make sure your hand is clearly visible to your camera and well-lit.
  2. Form the sign: Create the hand shape for the letter you want to communicate.
  3. Hold steady: Keep your hand position stable for a moment to allow the system to recognize it.
  4. Check the display: The recognized letter will appear in the "Detected Letter" section.
  5. Build words: Consecutive recognized letters will form words in the text output area.

Sign Language Alphabet

Practice these hand signs to communicate using the American Sign Language alphabet.

A
Close your hand into a fist with thumb to the side
B
Hold your hand up with fingers together and thumb tucked
C
Curve your hand into a C shape
D
Index finger pointed up, other fingers and thumb forming an O
E
Curl your fingers in toward palm with thumb tucked
F
Touch thumb to index, other fingers extended
G
Point index finger forward, thumb and other fingers extended
H
Extend index and middle fingers together
I
Make a fist with pinky finger extended
J
Extend pinky and trace a J shape
K
Index finger and middle finger form a V from thumb
L
Form an L shape with thumb and index finger
M
Three fingers tucked under thumb
N
Two fingers tucked under thumb
O
Form a circle with all fingertips and thumb
P
Point middle finger down with index extended
Q
Point index finger down with thumb and index making a G
R
Cross index and middle finger together
S
Make a fist with thumb wrapped over fingers
T
Thumb between index and middle finger
U
Extend index and middle finger up together
V
Make a V shape with index and middle finger
W
Extend index, middle, and ring fingers
X
Hook your index finger and thumb together
Y
Extend thumb and pinky, other fingers closed
Z
Draw a Z shape with your index finger

Practice these signs regularly to build your proficiency in American Sign Language.

Tips for Better Recognition

Follow these guidelines to improve the accuracy of our sign language detection system.

Good Lighting

Ensure your hand is well-lit from the front. Avoid backlighting or dim environments as they can reduce recognition accuracy.

Camera Position

Position your hand at the center of the camera frame, about 1-2 feet away from the camera. Make sure your entire hand is visible.

Hold Steady

Hold each sign steady for a few seconds to allow the system to recognize it accurately. Avoid rapid movements between signs.
